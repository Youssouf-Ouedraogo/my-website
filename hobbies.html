Long Distance running

Long distance running is not a common sport in Burkina Faso at least for where I grew up. From what I can remember the longest time I hard to run in high school was 12 minutes. For the national exams endurance running is 1.5 kilometers which is about a mile which I runned around 5.5 minutes. When I moved to the US. I realized that soccer is not a common sport as in Burkina Faso. That why and when I got interested in long distance running. Initially, I would just go out for a 2 to 3 mile (3 to 5 km) run. For my last semester at Northampton Community College, I joined the cross country team. Initially it was hard to keep up. With time I was able to better myself and I would go out for a 7 to 10 miles (11 to 16 km). For the competitions, we only competed a 3.15 miles (5 km) once the rest of our competition were 5 miles (8 km). My personal record for 3.15 miles is 19 minutes 20 seconds and for the 5 miles my personal record is 32 minutes 48 seconds. One main thing I learned about cross country is that is not just a sport it is a life philosophy. When I decided I was joining the cross country team, lot of people told me that it was hard. And, yes it is hard when I went to the trainings, for the first two miles I started questionning why I was doing this to myself. By the time a realized I am already done with the 7 to 10 miles. The same goes with life, when you are trying out something new, lot of people will tell you that is hard, and some others will tell you that you will not be able to make it. That is not what mostly matters. What mostly matters is your state of mindset to achieving your goals. Do you believe that you can achieve them or will you let the others have reasons over your. If you ask my opinion, I would say, Never let someone fulfill their bad prophecy on you.
